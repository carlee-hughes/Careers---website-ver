Sometimes it is difficult to decide where to get started. Fortunately, there are countless learning and experiential opportunities offered in the field of computer science. These events, internships and apprentice-like placements are great places to start if you're looking at how to get involved in the field. Co-Op Placement

Co-Op Placement with the University of Ottawa

Co-Op is a partnership between companies and students, where students take on work similar to that of an apprentice, in exchange for work experience and learning opportunities. An example of somewhere you could participate in a Co-Op program is the university of Ottawa (link), where you will be placed to work in workplaces, and gain credits and experience for the university, organized by it.

How to apply

According to the University of Ottawa's

  • You can apply to co-op jobs posted exclusively for University of Ottawa co-op students, via the CO-OP Navigator or the co-op app. You must participate in the "placement process" to apply to these jobs.
  • Apply to other potential jobs on external websites through the IndEx tool.
  • You can apply to jobs on the CO-OP Navigator / Co-op app

However, for more information about the application process, click here.

Benefits

Co-Op will allow you to gain real work experience, while still learning on the job and being taught. Although it is unlikely you will get paid, you will gain experience, credentials, and knowledge that will contribute to your credibility and desirability to have in a workplace. Co-Op is also a good, low-risk way to discover whether or not a field of study is really for you. Something may sound good on paper, but not in practice. Co-Op will also allow you to build relationships with the company or organization you do your Co-Op at, which may help you with recommendations and finding work later on, or even being employed by them.


Summer Internship

Future Tech Summer Internship at Nokia

You can take a summer internship at Nokia, in order to learn on-the-job and get real world work experience, a step up from Co-Op. The program is eight weeks, where you work alongside engineering teams in order to solve real problems for the company. The difference here is that while Co-Op typically goes through the school, and is organized and related directly to gaining credits, the Nokia internship program is separate from academics. However, you will learn extraordinary coding and computer science skills, and even get paid for your work.

How to apply

  • You must be registered in two of the following grade 12 courses: Physics, Chemistry, Advanced Functions, Calculus and Vectors, Computer Science, Biology, Earth and Space Science, Computer Engineering Technology, and Manufacturing Engineering Technology.
  • You have to make a video to apply, demonstrating a computer science related project you have made.

However, for more information about the application process, click here.

Benefits

You will learn from Nokia Future Tech Mentors while working on solving genuine technical and computer science related problems. It is an excellent opportunity to learn hands-on and on the job what it is like to properly work in the computer science industry. By doing the internship, you will gain valuable knowledge and skills, and build your work habits. It provides you with an opportunity to make connections with people working in the tech industry, and meet others close in age interested in the same topics. It is financially beneficial on top of all of this, as you will be paid a typical hourly (minimum) wage.


Career Fair

Discover Technata Career Fair

The Technata Career Fair is an event held to expose job seekers and those interested in computer science to potential employment and career opportunities. It is a good idea to attend if you’d like to know where to start. The fair is held at Brookstreet Hotel, 525 Legget Dr, Kanata, Ontario. There, inside, ‘some of the biggest names in Kanata North tech’ all conjoin under one roof.

How to apply

  • There is no application process for this event, as it is open for all!

However, for more details, click here.

Benefits

The career fair is a good opportunity to explore potential future pathways, in an efficient and timely manner. Instead of spending weeks researching different careers, you can sample and preview unique pathways, and meet experts in the field who can explain and answer your questions. Career fairs are excellent inspirations for your career, and you can discover your interests, likes and dislikes while participating in a fun community event!
